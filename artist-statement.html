Using materials like stone, clay, loam, and metal, Gea Kalkhof creates immersive installations that reflect ecological, political, and social concerns. Furthermore the work often explores the interplay between organic matter and industrial forms. Her practice emphasizes collaboration with non-human forces and challenges conventional hierarchies and production systems.

My sculptures are mostly living environments—formed through collaboration with materials, time, and elemental forces. I explore the thresholds between nature and industry, presence and memory. Each work resists fixed meaning, inviting collective interpretation and emphasizing the fragile interdependence between species, matter, and place.
